PLATFORM ACCESS & BOOKING FEE AUTHORIZATION AGREEMENT
Pickleball Court Booking System — Version 11 · Configurable Booking Fee Remittance Terms

Read the full agreement before signing. The Sign button unlocks only after you scroll to the bottom.

1. Parties

Platform Provider: [Platform Provider / System Owner Legal Name] ("Platform Provider"), the developer, system owner, and administrator of the Pickleball Court Booking System, a software platform that enables customers to discover and book pickleball courts online.

Court Owner: The individual or business identified by the account below ("Court Owner"), who independently owns, operates, and is solely responsible for the physical The Quadrant venue at the address configured for this client.

The Platform Provider does not own, manage, staff, price, or operate the physical court. The Platform Provider provides the online booking software and related platform services only.

2. What the Platform Provides

While this Agreement remains active, the Platform Provider grants the Court Owner access to the following at no setup fee, one-time fee, monthly subscription fee, hidden fee, or any other platform charge to the Court Owner:

  • Online court booking page accessible to the public
  • Real-time slot availability management
  • Automated GCash receipt fraud detection and verification
  • Admin dashboard for managing bookings, courts, and staff accounts
  • Automated customer notifications and booking references
  • Platform hosting, security, and technical maintenance

3. Booking Fee & Monthly Remittance

In consideration of the platform access provided at no setup fee, one-time fee, monthly subscription fee, hidden fee, or other platform charge to the Court Owner, the Court Owner authorizes the Platform Provider to add the current System Owner-selected non-refundable booking fee to each customer transaction processed through the system. This fee is charged to and paid by the customer/player, not the Court Owner, and is separate from the court rental fee.

The System Owner sets the booking fee amount and charging method. The fee may be charged per booked hour per confirmed booking transaction or as a per confirmed booking transaction fee, added to the player's checkout, and included in the booking-period remittance statement based on booking date.

When it applies. The configured booking fee applies only to a confirmed booking transaction made through the platform, meaning a booking that successfully reserved a court slot. Bookings that are cancelled before confirmation, rejected during payment verification, expired, or never held a slot are not charged.

Collection. The Court Owner collects the configured booking fee from the customer together with the court rental fee at the time of booking, and holds the booking fee on behalf of the Platform Provider until remittance. The booking fee is not a fee charged to the Court Owner and is not part of the Court Owner's court rental revenue.

Monthly remittance. Remittance is settled monthly. Each month the Platform Provider issues a statement covering confirmed booking transactions made during the previous booking calendar month, regardless of the future court play date. The amount to remit is calculated using the System Owner-selected booking fee amount and charging method in effect when the statement is generated. Each confirmed booking transaction is included only once.

Due date & method. Remittance is due within five (5) calendar days of the statement date. The Court Owner remits that amount via GCash to the booking fee remittance account shown in the dashboard, then submits the GCash transaction reference and a screenshot of the receipt as remittance proof through the "Remit Now" function.

Non-remittance. Statements not remitted past the due date are marked overdue. The Platform Provider may suspend or restrict the Court Owner's access to the booking system until all outstanding statements are settled. Suspension does not waive any amounts already due.

4. Court Owner's Revenue

All court rental fees set by the Court Owner (e.g. ₱200/hr, ₱250/hr) belong exclusively to the Court Owner. The Platform Provider does not take any share of the court rental revenue. The Court Owner receives 100% of the court rental rate from every confirmed booking. Only the separate configured booking fee paid by the customer is for the Platform Provider.

5. Anti-Circumvention

The Court Owner agrees not to direct, encourage, or assist customers to bypass the platform in order to avoid the configured booking fee. This includes but is not limited to: advertising a direct booking channel at a lower price for platform-visible slots, accepting bookings verbally or via personal chat for slots available on the platform, waiving or hiding the booking fee outside the system, or instructing customers to "book directly" to avoid the fee.

Circumvention of the booking fee is a material breach of this Agreement and may result in immediate suspension or termination of platform access.

6. Obligation to Honor Bookings

The Court Owner agrees to honor all confirmed bookings made through the platform. Customers who receive a booking confirmation with a reference number must be allowed access to the court at the reserved date and time. Cancellations caused by the Court Owner's failure to honor a confirmed booking are a breach of this Agreement.

7. Court Owner's Responsibilities

  • Keep court availability, rates, and operating hours accurate on the platform
  • Manage and respond to customer concerns about the physical court experience
  • Ensure the court is safe, operational, and accessible during booked hours
  • Keep remittance references and receipt screenshots accurate and truthful
  • Keep admin login credentials confidential and not share access with unauthorized persons
  • Not export or misuse customer data for purposes unrelated to court management

8. Independence of Parties

The Court Owner and Platform Provider are fully independent parties. This Agreement does not create a partnership, franchise, joint venture, employment, or agency relationship. The Platform Provider is not responsible for:

  • Disputes between the Court Owner and customers
  • Physical injuries or accidents at the court
  • Loss of revenue due to court downtime or customer cancellations
  • Any business, tax, or legal obligations of the Court Owner

9. Platform Availability

The Platform Provider will make reasonable efforts to keep the system available. However, the Platform Provider does not guarantee 100% uptime and is not liable for losses caused by temporary platform outages, third-party service interruptions (e.g. Supabase, Cloudflare), or force majeure events.

10. Termination

Either party may terminate this Agreement with 14 days written notice by email or another written channel recorded by both parties. The Platform Provider may suspend access or terminate immediately if the Court Owner breaches Sections 5 (Anti-Circumvention) or 6 (Honor Bookings), fails to remit overdue booking fees, engages in fraud, or misuses customer data.
